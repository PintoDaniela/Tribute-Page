Iroh

About Iroh

Uncle Iroh, also known as General Iroh and referred to simply as Uncle, is a beloved fictional character in Nickelodeon's acclaimed animated television series Avatar: The Last Airbender. Created by Michael Dante DiMartino and Bryan Konietzko, the character was originally voiced by the late Mako in the first two seasons, and later by Greg Baldwin in the third season and the sequel series, The Legend of Korra.
In the episode "The Tales of Ba Sing Se", Iroh's name was written as 艾洛 (aì lùo) as a tribute to Mako, who had passed away months before the episode aired. The episode was dedicated "In Honor of Mako", highlighting the impact that he and his portrayal of Uncle Iroh had on the show's fans.

The Dragon of the West

A Backstory of Iroh's Life before The Last Airbender

Before the events of The Last Airbender, Iroh was the eldest son of Fire Lord Azulon. However, after his son Lu Ten died during the failed Fire Nation siege of Ba Sing Se, Iroh, who had always been motivated more by duty than personal ambition, retreated from military affairs. Despite having once envisioned himself capturing the city, he abandoned the siege following his son's death.
Iroh's younger brother, Ozai, was more ambitious and opportunistic. He attempted to convince their father that, due to Iroh's defeat and lack of heirs, Ozai should become the next Fire Lord instead of Iroh. But Azulon was outraged by this lack of respect for Iroh's position and ordered Ozai to kill his own son, Prince Zuko, as punishment. Ozai and his wife, Princess Ursa, conspired to assassinate Azulon instead, with Ursa accepting banishment for her role.
After Azulon's death, Iroh was mourning and disinterested in state affairs, allowing Ozai to seize the leadership of the Fire Nation. Although Iroh was believed to have killed the world's last dragons, he actually learned from the last two dragons, Ran and Shao, to derive his firebending powers from vitality rather than rage. He lied about killing the last of the dragons to end the Fire Nation tradition of dragon hunts. Iroh was also a high-ranking member (Grand Lotus) of the Order of the White Lotus, an international secret society.

Plot overview

  • Book One: Iroh accompanies his nephew Zuko in his mission to capture the Avatar, who threatens the Fire Nation's conquest. Iroh learns of Admiral Zhao's plan to kill the moon spirit, disrupt the natural order, and attacks him. He is named a traitor.
  • Book Two: Iroh and Zuko are fugitives. Iroh teaches Zuko a waterbending-inspired technique to redirect lightning. They take refuge in Ba Sing Se and operate a tea-house. Azula betrays them, and Iroh is arrested.
  • Book Three: Iroh fakes madness while preparing for the solar eclipse, during which Firebending does not work. He escapes his cell, and in the series finale, he calls the White Lotus to free Ba Sing Se. After the war ends, Iroh offers the Fire Lord's throne to Zuko and returns to his tea shop in Ba Sing Se.
  • Comic book sequel: In The Promise, Iroh advises Aang and Zuko on dealing with the Harmony Restoration Movement, invents bubble tea, and in The Search, becomes acting Firelord while Zuko searches for his mother.
  • The Legend of Korra: Iroh uses astral projection to become a resident of the Spirit World. He aids Korra when she is trapped in the Spirit World and provides hints to Aang's children about Jinora's location. He also advises Korra to seek Zuko's counsel when facing a new threat.
  • Zuko's grandson: Named Iroh, he becomes a general of the United Forces, a multi-ethnic nation founded by Aang and Zuko after the Hundred Years' War.
